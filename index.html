A family-authorized international cultural archive project.

English

This online museum and archival project is established with the authorization and support of Gu Yuan's family.

The project is developed in collaboration with Gu Ancun, daughter of the renowned Chinese printmaking master Gu Yuan, and supports the cultural and artistic continuation of the Gu Yuan artistic legacy in Toronto, Canada.

The initiative also supports Gu Yuan's granddaughter Duan Wei and her four children in preserving, promoting, researching, and developing the international cultural presence of Gu Yuan's art for future generations.

This platform aims to serve as:

  • A digital museum and research archive
  • An educational and cultural platform
  • A bridge between Chinese printmaking history and international audiences
  • A future foundation for exhibitions, publications, educational programs, and cultural exchange in Canada

繁體中文

本線上美術館與數位檔案館項目，經古元家族正式授權與支持建立。

本項目由中國著名版畫藝術大師「古元」之女古安村女士協力支持，並致力於在加拿大多倫多推動古元藝術文化的國際傳承與發展。

同時，本項目亦支持古元外孫女段薇女士與其四位子女，共同參與古元藝術的保存、研究、推廣與未來國際文化發展工作。

本平台未來將作為：

  • 古元藝術數位美術館與研究檔案庫
  • 國際藝術教育與文化交流平台
  • 中國新興版畫歷史的重要海外展示窗口
  • 未來展覽、出版、學術研究與加拿大文化推廣的重要基礎

Artist Timeline

A clean chronological pathway helps visitors understand Gu Yuan's movement from Guangdong to Yan'an, from revolutionary printmaking to national art education.

1919Born in Guangdong, China
1938Went to Yan'an and entered the revolutionary art environment
1940Graduated from Lu Xun Academy of Literature and Art
1940sCreated defining Yan'an woodcuts rooted in local life
1959Joined Central Academy of Fine Arts as an educator
